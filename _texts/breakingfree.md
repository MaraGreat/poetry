title: Breaking Free
---
(Gallaudet University, April 2023) 

- On the black stage, 
- you fluently glide, jump and fly. 
- Embodying rhythms, you tap into 
- your pulse and heartbeat.
- Internally, you count steps 
- to flow, whirl, and land 
- firmly. Defying 
- the common notion:
- *dancing is not for the deaf!*

- Your performance moved me 
- to tears. Your song sings, 
- “I feel no fears.” 

- “Wear ear plugs!” you warned.
- I shield my ear drums 
- from deafening pulses and thumps,
- while my soul embraces 
- your full-on freedom.
  
- After the show
- you dance 
- with words.  
- Hands and arms 
- sing; vibrant expressions convey 
- connection and community.

- At your age I was heavily draped 
- in depressing dogmas: 
- Dance was from the devil, 
- freedom was frightening.
- The greatest virtue –
- sitting still. Punishing pews
- testing my faith.
